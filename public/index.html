- Each player takes turn in DRAFT, ATTACK, and FORTIFY phases in that order.
- In DRAFT phase, you are rewarded a number of troops based on the number of territories and continents you control. You then choose territory(s) to deploy them in to strengthen your forces. The troops can be spread as concentrated or as thinly as you plan to.
- In ATTACK phase, you can initiate attacks from any of your controlled territories to a neighbouring enemy territory. Each territory must have 1 troop remaining to stand the ground, so you always attack with 1 less troop than you control. In each attack, an attacker can attack with 3 troops at most, whereas the defender can defend with 2 only. Each troop translates to a roll of dice. The highest-valued roll of the attacker battles with the highest-valued roll of the defender, and so on. The lower-valued of the two loses the battle, and the corresponding troop is eliminated. In the case of a draw, the attacker troop is eliminated. If all defender troops are eliminated, you can select how many of your troops to move to the newly controlled territory. Attacks can happen as many times as possible per phase, as long as you have the troops to back it.
- In FORTIFY phase, you can choose to move troops from a controlled territory to a connected controlled territory. Unlike in DRAFT phase, this can only be done once per phase, so plan wisely.
- If you have newly taken control of an enemy territory in ATTACK phase, you will be rewarded a card, 1 of 3 types, at the end of your turn. You can redeem these cards in sets of 3's during your DRAFT phase, in exchange for additional draft troops. You can only redeem 3-of-a-kinds, or 3-of-all-different-kinds.
- When the entire map is controlled by 1 player only, the game ends in that player's victory.
